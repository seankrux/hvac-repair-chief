# HVAC Maintenance FAQs -- Boca Raton, FL

## How often should I have my HVAC system serviced in South Florida?

We recommend twice per year for South Florida systems -- once before the summer cooling season (March or April) and once before the winter heating season (October or November). Because Boca Raton systems run year-round, semi-annual maintenance is more appropriate than the once-per-year standard for colder climates.

## What is the difference between a tune-up and a maintenance plan?

A one-time tune-up covers a single visit. A maintenance plan is an ongoing agreement that includes two or more visits per year, priority scheduling, and discounts on repairs. A plan is the better value for homeowners who want consistent protection.

## How do I know if my system needs maintenance?

Signs include higher energy bills without obvious cause, the system running longer to reach temperature, reduced airflow from vents, increased humidity indoors, or any unusual sounds or odors. But the best approach is scheduled maintenance before problems become apparent.

## How often should I change my air filter?

In South Florida, most homes should change standard 1-inch filters every 30 to 60 days. High-traffic homes with pets may need monthly changes. Thicker media filters can last 3 to 6 months. Our technicians will advise on the right schedule for your specific system during a maintenance visit.

## Does maintenance include refrigerant?

A standard maintenance visit includes checking refrigerant pressure. If levels are low, we diagnose the cause -- low refrigerant always means a leak. Refrigerant addition is a separate charge and requires repair of the leak.

## Can I do any maintenance myself?

Yes. Changing or cleaning filters regularly is the most impactful thing a homeowner can do. Keeping the outdoor condenser unit clear of debris and plants also helps. Everything else -- electrical testing, refrigerant checks, coil cleaning -- should be done by a licensed technician.

## Will maintenance prevent all breakdowns?

Regular maintenance dramatically reduces the risk of breakdowns but cannot guarantee them entirely. Components can fail unpredictably. However, maintenance customers typically face fewer, less severe, and less costly repair situations than those who skip regular service.
