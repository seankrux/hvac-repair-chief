# Heating Repair in Boca Raton, FL

South Florida winters are mild by national standards, but when temperatures drop into the 40s and 50s, a properly functioning heating system matters. Most Boca Raton homes use heat pump systems that provide both cooling and heating. When your heat pump fails to heat on a cool South Florida evening, HVAC Repair Chief provides prompt, same-day heating repair service.

## Heating Systems We Service

**Heat Pumps (Most Common in South Florida)**
The vast majority of Boca Raton homes rely on heat pump systems. In heating mode, the heat pump reverses its refrigerant cycle to extract heat from outdoor air and deliver it indoors. Common heat pump heating failures include:

- Reversing valve failure (most common heat-specific failure)
- Defrost board issues (prevents ice buildup on outdoor coil)
- Refrigerant problems affecting heating capacity
- Compressor failures affecting both heating and cooling
- Thermostat configuration errors that prevent heating mode

**Electric Strip Heat (Emergency / Supplemental)**
Many South Florida air handlers include electric resistance heating strips as backup or supplemental heat. Issues include failed heating elements, blown thermal fuses, and control board failures.

**Gas Furnaces (Less Common, Some Older Homes)**
Some older South Florida homes have gas furnaces. We service these systems including heat exchanger inspection, igniter replacement, gas valve issues, and blower motor failures.

## Signs Your Heating System Needs Repair

- System switches to emergency heat mode automatically (indicates heat pump problem)
- Blowing cool air when heating mode is selected
- Outdoor unit running in heating mode but home stays cold
- System won't enter heating mode at all
- Outdoor unit covered in ice and not defrosting
- Unusually high electric bills during heating season (may indicate strip heat running constantly)

## South Florida Heating Considerations

Heat pumps become less efficient in very cold air - generally below 40 degrees. Since South Florida rarely drops below this threshold, heat pumps are ideal for our climate. However, on the rare extremely cold nights (below 40 degrees), your system may automatically engage supplemental electric heat, which is normal.

If your heat pump is running constantly during cold weather and still not heating adequately, the issue may be a refrigerant problem, a reversing valve failure, or the system being undersized for heating mode.
