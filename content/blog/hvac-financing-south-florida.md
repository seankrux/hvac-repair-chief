# HVAC Financing in South Florida: Options for Every Budget

Your air conditioner dies on a Tuesday in August. It's 94°F outside. Replacing it costs $8,000. Most homeowners don't have that sitting in a checking account—and waiting for funds isn't an option in South Florida's heat.

The good news: there are solid HVAC financing options for every credit profile and situation. Here's what's available to Boca Raton homeowners.

## Option 1: Contractor Financing

Most established HVAC companies in South Florida offer financing through third-party lenders (GreenSky, Synchrony, Wells Fargo Home Projects, etc.). This is often the fastest path to installation.

**How it works:**
- Apply at point of sale (often instant approval)
- Funds go directly to the contractor
- You make monthly payments to the lender

**Common terms:**
- 0% promotional periods for 12, 18, or 24 months (deferred interest—pay it off before the period ends)
- Fixed-rate loans at 5–12% APR for 5–10 year terms
- Same-as-cash options with no payment for 90 days

**Watch out for deferred interest:** The "0% for 18 months" offers often accrue interest from day one—if you don't pay the full balance by the end of the promotional period, all the interest is added back. Read the fine print carefully.

## Option 2: FPL On-Bill Financing

Florida Power & Light offers the **On Bill Finance (OBF) program** for qualifying customers replacing HVAC equipment with high-efficiency models. The loan is repaid through your monthly electric bill.

**Key features:**
- No credit check for loans under $10,000
- Competitive fixed interest rates
- Financing tied to the meter (not the customer), so it transfers with the home if you sell
- Must use an FPL-approved contractor

This is one of the strongest financing options available for South Florida homeowners—particularly for those with credit challenges.

## Option 3: PACE Financing (Property Assessed Clean Energy)

PACE financing is a Florida-specific program that allows homeowners to finance energy-efficient home improvements (including HVAC) through a special assessment on their property tax bill.

**How it works:**
- Financing amount added to annual property tax
- Paid over 5–25 years as part of your tax bill
- Transfers to new owner if you sell (this is both an advantage and a consideration for buyers)
- No minimum credit score for most programs

**Florida PACE providers:** Ygrene, FortiFi, and others operate in Palm Beach and Broward counties.

**Caution:** PACE liens are in senior position relative to your mortgage on some defaults—understand the terms before proceeding. PACE is best suited for long-term homeowners.

## Option 4: Home Equity Loan or HELOC

If you have equity in your Boca Raton home, a home equity loan or line of credit (HELOC) typically offers the lowest interest rates:

| Loan Type | Rate Range | Best For |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| HELOC | Prime + 0–2% (currently ~8–10%) | Flexible needs |
| Home equity loan | 7–9% fixed | Known, fixed cost |
| Cash-out refinance | 6–8% fixed | Major renovation + HVAC |

Interest on home equity loans used for home improvements may be tax-deductible—consult your tax advisor.

## Option 5: Personal Loans

For homeowners without significant equity, personal loans from banks, credit unions, or online lenders provide another option:

- Rates vary widely: 7–25%+ depending on credit
- No collateral required
- Faster than home equity options (often 1–3 business days)
- Loan amounts up to $40,000+

Credit unions often offer better rates than traditional banks for personal loans. If you're a member of a Florida-based credit union, check their rates first.

## Option 6: Credit Cards (Use Carefully)

A 0% APR promotional credit card can work for shorter-term financing if you're confident you can pay off the balance during the promotional period:

- Many cards offer 15–21 month 0% intro periods
- Must have good to excellent credit
- Minimum monthly payments required
- Rates spike to 20–28% after the promotional period

This option works well for smaller repairs ($1,000–$3,000) that you can pay off quickly, less well for full system replacements.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What credit score do I need to finance HVAC in South Florida?**
A: Most contractor financing programs approve applicants with credit scores of 640+. FPL On-Bill Finance and PACE programs require no minimum credit score. For the best rates, a score of 720+ is ideal.

**Q: Can I finance HVAC if I have bad credit?**
A: Yes, through FPL's On-Bill Finance program (no credit check under $10,000) or PACE financing. Some contractor lenders also offer second-look programs for applicants initially declined.

**Q: How long can I finance a new HVAC system?**
A: Terms typically range from 12 months (0% promo) to 15 years (for larger PACE or home equity loans). Match the loan term to a period you're comfortable paying—longer terms mean lower monthly payments but more total interest paid.

**Q: Will HVAC financing affect my home sale?**
A: Contractor loans and personal loans don't affect the home sale. PACE loans attach to the property and transfer to the buyer—disclose these to buyers and understand buyer pushback is common.
