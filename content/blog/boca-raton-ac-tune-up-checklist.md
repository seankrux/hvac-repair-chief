# The Complete AC Tune-Up Checklist for Boca Raton Homeowners

In Boca Raton, your air conditioner isn't a seasonal luxury—it's a year-round necessity. That makes annual (and ideally semi-annual) tune-ups not just smart maintenance, but essential protection for one of your home's most expensive systems.

This checklist walks through exactly what a professional AC tune-up should include, what you can do yourself between service visits, and how to know if your technician is doing the job right.

## Why Tune-Ups Matter More in South Florida

The math is straightforward: a Boca Raton air conditioner runs roughly 2,800–3,200 hours per year. A comparable system in New York might run 1,000–1,200 hours. That's nearly triple the wear. Without regular maintenance:

- Efficiency drops 5–10% annually as dirt and debris accumulate
- Small issues become expensive failures during peak summer heat
- Refrigerant leaks go undetected until cooling fails completely
- Drain lines clog with algae, causing water damage and shutdowns

A tune-up typically costs $75–$150 and can prevent $500–$3,000+ in repair bills.

## What a Professional AC Tune-Up Should Include

### Electrical System Checks

- [ ] Test and record voltage and amperage on all motors
- [ ] Inspect and tighten electrical connections
- [ ] Test capacitors (start and run) — a common failure point in Florida heat
- [ ] Check contactor for pitting or burning
- [ ] Verify disconnect box and breaker sizing

### Refrigerant System

- [ ] Check refrigerant levels and test for leaks
- [ ] Inspect refrigerant lines for damage, kinking, or insulation wear
- [ ] Measure superheat and subcooling to verify system charge

### Coil Cleaning

- [ ] Clean evaporator coil (indoor) — algae and mold growth is common in South Florida
- [ ] Clean condenser coil (outdoor) — should be rinsed with water to remove salt deposits, pollen, and debris
- [ ] Straighten bent fins on both coils

### Drain System

- [ ] Clear the condensate drain line with a wet-vac or pressurized nitrogen
- [ ] Treat drain pan with algae tablets
- [ ] Verify secondary drain and float switch operation

### Air Handling and Filtration

- [ ] Replace or inspect air filter
- [ ] Check blower wheel for dirt buildup
- [ ] Measure airflow across evaporator coil
- [ ] Lubricate blower motor bearings (if applicable)

### Thermostat and Controls

- [ ] Calibrate thermostat settings
- [ ] Test heating and cooling modes
- [ ] Verify safety controls and shutoffs

## DIY Maintenance Between Professional Visits

| Task | Frequency | Difficulty |
|------|-----------|------------|
| Change air filter | Every 30–60 days | Easy |
| Pour diluted bleach down drain line | Monthly | Easy |
| Rinse condenser unit with garden hose | Monthly | Easy |
| Clear debris from around outdoor unit | Weekly | Easy |
| Check thermostat batteries | Every 6 months | Easy |

Keep at least 2 feet of clearance around your outdoor condenser. In South Florida, plants grow fast—trim back shrubs seasonally.

## How to Evaluate Your Technician's Work

A quality tune-up includes written documentation. Ask for:

- **Before/after refrigerant readings** — Technician should record system pressures
- **Voltage and amperage measurements** — Written record of each motor
- **Itemized report** — List every item checked, not just a generic invoice

If a technician shows up, spends 20 minutes, and hands you a bill—ask questions. A thorough tune-up takes 45–90 minutes.

## When to Schedule Your Tune-Up

The best times in South Florida:

- **Late February/March** — Before the summer rush; technicians have availability and you catch issues before peak demand
- **October/November** — Good for checking heating mode and preparing for cooler months

Avoid scheduling during June–August when demand is highest and appointment windows are limited.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How often should I get an AC tune-up in Boca Raton?**
A: Due to South Florida's year-round runtime, we recommend twice per year—once in early spring before peak summer and once in fall. At minimum, once annually.

**Q: What's included in an AC tune-up vs. a repair visit?**
A: A tune-up is preventative maintenance—cleaning, testing, and adjusting a functioning system. A repair visit addresses a specific failure or symptom. Tune-ups often catch issues before they become repair calls.

**Q: Can I do an AC tune-up myself?**
A: Homeowners can handle filter changes, drain line maintenance, and condenser rinsing. Refrigerant checks, electrical testing, and coil cleaning require licensed HVAC technicians in Florida.

**Q: What does an AC tune-up cost in Boca Raton?**
A: Professional tune-ups typically range from $75 to $150 for a single visit. Many HVAC companies offer annual maintenance plans that include two tune-ups plus discounts on repairs for $150–$300/year.
