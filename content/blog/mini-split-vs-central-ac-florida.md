# Mini-Split vs. Central AC: Which Is Better for South Florida Homes?

The air conditioning market has changed dramatically over the last decade. Mini-split systems—once primarily found in commercial spaces and vacation rentals—have become a mainstream option for South Florida homeowners. But are they actually better than central AC for Boca Raton's climate and housing stock?

The answer depends on your home, your budget, and your priorities. Here's an honest comparison.

## How Each System Works

**Central AC** uses a network of ducts to distribute conditioned air throughout the home from a single air handler. A single thermostat (or zone controller) manages the whole system.

**Mini-split (ductless)** delivers conditioned air directly from wall-mounted indoor units (heads) to individual rooms, without ductwork. Each head has its own temperature control, and one outdoor unit can serve multiple indoor heads (multi-zone systems).

## Head-to-Head Comparison

| Factor | Central AC | Mini-Split |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| Upfront cost (average home) | $5,000–$12,000 | $3,000–$10,000+ (varies by zones) |
| Installation complexity | Requires ductwork | No ducts; easier retrofit |
| Efficiency (SEER) | 14–22 SEER | 18–30+ SEER |
| Individual room control | Limited (with zones) | Excellent |
| Aesthetics | Invisible (vents only) | Wall units visible |
| Maintenance | Duct cleaning needed | Monthly filter cleaning |
| Best for | Homes with existing ducts | Additions, older homes, high efficiency |

## When Mini-Splits Win in South Florida

### Home Additions and Garage Conversions

Extending ductwork to a new addition is expensive ($3,000–$8,000+) and often impractical in slab-on-grade construction. A single-zone mini-split for an addition or converted space typically runs $2,000–$4,000 installed—a clear winner.

### Older Homes Without Ductwork

Many South Florida homes built before the 1970s have no ductwork at all, or have inadequate ductwork. Installing new ducts throughout an older home is a major renovation project. Mini-splits eliminate that cost entirely.

### Maximum Efficiency Priority

The highest-efficiency mini-splits from Daikin, Mitsubishi, and LG achieve SEER ratings of 20–30+. No central system approaches that level. If reducing energy consumption is your top priority, mini-splits have the edge.

### Precise Zone Control

Mini-splits let every room maintain its own temperature independently. If your household has wildly different temperature preferences, or if you want to condition only occupied rooms, mini-splits provide flexibility central systems can't match without expensive zone control upgrades.

## When Central AC Wins in South Florida

### Homes with Existing Ductwork in Good Condition

If your home already has well-maintained ductwork, installing a new central system is almost always cheaper than ripping it out and installing multiple mini-split heads throughout the house.

### Whole-Home Comfort with Consistent Aesthetics

Many homeowners simply prefer the invisible footprint of central AC. Vent grilles are subtle; wall-mounted mini-split heads are more prominent—acceptable in some rooms, less so in others.

### Straightforward Single-Zone Cooling

For a typical 3-bedroom, 2-bath Boca Raton home with functional ductwork, a central system provides all the cooling needed without the complexity and cost of multiple mini-split zones.

## The Hybrid Approach

Many South Florida homeowners use both: a central system for the main living areas and mini-splits for specific problem areas—a Florida room, a detached workshop, an in-law suite, or a bonus room over the garage. This hybrid approach often provides the best overall value.

## Cost to Install Mini-Splits in South Florida

| Configuration | Typical Installed Cost |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Single-zone (1 indoor head) | $2,000–$4,500 |
| Dual-zone (2 heads, 1 outdoor unit) | $4,500–$8,000 |
| Tri-zone (3 heads, 1 outdoor unit) | $7,000–$12,000 |
| Quad-zone (4 heads) | $9,000–$15,000 |

For whole-home replacement of a 4-bedroom house, multi-zone mini-splits may cost more than a new central system. But you'll save on duct maintenance and enjoy superior efficiency.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can mini-splits handle the humidity in South Florida?**
A: Yes—modern mini-splits have excellent dehumidification capability. Some models include a dedicated "dry mode" for humidity control without significant temperature drop.

**Q: Do mini-splits work well in Florida heat?**
A: Absolutely. Mini-splits are used throughout Florida and throughout much hotter climates globally. They're extremely common in Japan, Singapore, and Southeast Asia—climates similar to South Florida.

**Q: Which brands are best for South Florida mini-splits?**
A: Mitsubishi (Mr. Slim), Daikin, LG, and Fujitsu are all proven performers in South Florida's climate. All offer strong corrosion resistance and high SEER ratings.

**Q: Do I need permits for a mini-split installation in Boca Raton?**
A: Yes. Florida requires permits for mini-split installations, and a licensed HVAC contractor must perform the work. Unpermitted HVAC work can affect homeowner's insurance and create problems at resale.
