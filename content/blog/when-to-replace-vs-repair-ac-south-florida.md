# AC Repair vs. Replacement: How to Decide in South Florida's Climate

Your air conditioner just broke down—again. The repair quote is $800. The replacement quote is $9,000. What do you do?

For South Florida homeowners, this decision carries higher stakes than almost anywhere else in the country. Your AC isn't a seasonal comfort item; it's infrastructure. Making the wrong call can mean thousands in wasted repair costs on a dying system, or paying for a new unit prematurely.

Here's a clear framework for making the right decision.

## The $5,000 Rule (Modified for South Florida)

The traditional "5,000 rule" says: multiply the repair cost by the unit's age. If the result exceeds $5,000, replace instead of repair.

**Example:** $400 repair × 12 years old = $4,800 → Borderline, lean toward replacement  
**Example:** $300 repair × 8 years old = $2,400 → Repair makes sense

However, South Florida's climate warrants a modification: **use $4,000 as your threshold**, not $5,000. Why? Because our systems age faster due to year-round runtime and humidity stress. A 10-year-old system in Boca Raton is closer to the end of its useful life than a 10-year-old system in Chicago.

## Age: The Most Important Factor

| System Age | Recommendation |
|------------|----------------|
| Under 7 years | Repair unless catastrophic failure |
| 7–10 years | Evaluate with modified 5,000 rule |
| 10–13 years | Strongly lean toward replacement |
| 13+ years | Replace in almost all cases |

South Florida HVAC systems typically last 10–15 years. If your system is past the 10-year mark and facing a major repair, you're likely throwing good money after bad.

## Repair Costs That Justify Replacement

Some failures are inherently replacement signals:

- **Compressor failure** — The compressor is the most expensive component. Replacement costs $1,500–$2,500 for the part alone. On a system over 8 years old, replacement is almost always smarter.
- **Refrigerant (R-22) leak in older systems** — R-22 (Freon) was phased out in 2020. Recharging an R-22 system now costs $100–$150 per pound and the supply is limited. This is a strong replacement signal.
- **Heat exchanger crack** — Applies to gas furnaces. A cracked heat exchanger is a safety issue and typically warrants replacement of the entire unit.
- **Multiple failures within 12 months** — If you've repaired the system twice this year, it's showing end-of-life patterns.

## When Repair Is Clearly the Right Choice

Not every breakdown signals replacement. Repair confidently when:

- The system is under 7 years old
- The failure is a minor component (capacitor, contactor, relay, fan motor)
- The repair cost is under $500
- The system has been well-maintained and runs efficiently

Common low-cost repairs in South Florida:

| Repair | Typical Cost | Replace? |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Capacitor replacement | $150–$300 | No |
| Contactor replacement | $150–$250 | No |
| Drain line clearing | $75–$150 | No |
| Refrigerant recharge (R-410A) | $200–$400 | Only if 10+ years old |
| Fan motor replacement | $300–$600 | Depends on age |
| Compressor replacement | $1,500–$2,500 | Almost always yes if 8+ years |

## Hidden Costs of Keeping an Old System

Beyond repair bills, aging systems cost more every month:

- A 12-year-old SEER 10 system running in Boca Raton costs significantly more to operate than a new SEER 18 unit
- The energy savings from a new system can offset 30–50% of the replacement cost over 5–7 years
- Old systems provide inadequate dehumidification, forcing your home to feel hotter than the thermostat reading

## Getting an Honest Assessment

The challenge: HVAC contractors sometimes recommend replacement when repair would suffice (higher ticket), and sometimes push repairs on systems that should be replaced (quick win, return customer). Get at least two quotes for any repair over $500, and ask directly: "If this were your home, would you repair or replace?"

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is it worth repairing an AC that is 10 years old in South Florida?**
A: It depends on the repair cost and what's failed. Minor repairs under $300 on a 10-year-old system may still make sense. Major repairs over $800, especially compressor or coil failures, usually don't pencil out.

**Q: What is the average cost to replace an AC system in Boca Raton?**
A: Full system replacement (air handler + condenser + installation) typically costs $6,000–$14,000 depending on home size, system efficiency rating, and ductwork condition.

**Q: How do I know if my AC is R-22 or R-410A?**
A: Check the data plate on the outdoor condenser unit. It will list the refrigerant type. You can also call us and provide the model number — we can look it up instantly.

**Q: Can I just replace the outdoor unit and keep my old air handler?**
A: Sometimes, but it's risky. Mismatched systems don't operate at rated efficiency and can void warranties. If the air handler is also aging, replacing both together costs less than doing them separately.
